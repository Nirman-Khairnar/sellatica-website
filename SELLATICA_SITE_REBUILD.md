## 6. COPYWRITING RULES — FOLLOW STRICTLY

This site must read like it was written by a world-class B2B copywriter. Not a content marketer. Not an AI. A writer who understands that in B2B, the person reading your copy is exhausted, sceptical, and has been burned by consultants before.

### Voice
- Direct. No hedging.
- Specific. Never vague.
- First-person where it fits (Nirman is the author, the operator, the person they are hiring).
- Confident without being arrogant. Proven without being boastful.
- Short sentences when making a point. Longer ones when building context.

### What to never write
- "We are committed to..." → says nothing
- "Leveraging cutting-edge AI..." → says nothing
- "Transform your business..." → everyone says this
- "Seamless integration..." → meaningless
- "World-class solutions..." → sounds fake
- "Unlock your potential..." → marketing garbage
- Any sentence that could apply to any other company on earth

### What to always write instead
- Specific outcomes: "50 calls became 11"
- Honest mechanics: "I find 3–5 places in your business where work has no clear path, no owner, and no automatic next step"
- Real timeframes: "break-even in under 4 weeks"
- The client's actual words if you have them

### Rhythm
Vary it. Short punchy lines. Then a longer sentence that builds. Then short again. Never three sentences the same length back to back.

### Anti-AI writing patterns to avoid in all copy
- Do not use: "stands as", "serves as", "underscores", "reflects broader", "showcasing", "highlighting", "fostering", "encompassing"
- Do not use em dashes to fake depth
- Do not end sections with: "The future looks bright" / "Exciting times ahead" / "Let us help you get there"
- Do not use: "groundbreaking", "transformative", "revolutionary", "game-changing", "pioneering"
- Do not use hollow triples: "streamline, optimise, and scale" — pick the specific one that applies
